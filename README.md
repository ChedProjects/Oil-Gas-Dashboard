# Oil & Gas Production & Revenue Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)
Author: Cheddy Emile Tools Used: Excel, Power Query, Microsoft Power BI, DAX

# Datasets 
OGORBcsv.csv — Raw Monthly U.S. Federal Oil & Gas Production & Disposition Data (2015–2025). Real-oil-price.csv - Historical U.S. WTI Oil Pricing Data (1986–2026) Personal-Project.csv — Combined and transformed dataset used for dashboard modeling and analysis Overview.

# Overview
This project analyzes U.S. oil & gas production trends, commodity pricing, and estimated revenue performance using publicly available operational datasets and historical WTI crude oil pricing data.Oil production data was sourced from publicly available datasets, while oil pricing data was obtained from the U.S. Energy Information Administration.
# Dashboard Features
Excel and Power Query were used for data cleaning, transformation, and integration, while Power BI was used to develop an executive-level business intelligence dashboard.The dashboard was designed to support financial, operational, and strategic decision-making within the energy sector through KPI reporting, geographic analysis, time-series modeling, and revenue trend analysis.

# Key Features

Executive Summary dashboard with KPI cards and high-level operational insights
Geographic analysis of production volume by state
Revenue and production trend analysis
Weighted WTI pricing analysis by production region
Year-over-Year growth modeling using DAX time intelligence
Commodity mix breakdown (Gas vs Oil)
Revenue modeling based on production volume and WTI pricing
Disposition category analysis and operational reporting
Interactive filtering and executive-style visualizations
State-level weighted average WTI pricing was used to model estimated revenue performance across production regions.

# Executive Summary 

Core Business Insights Total Revenue Analyzed: $3.13 trillion Total Production Volume: 41 billion units Commodity Mix: Gas = 90.84% Oil = 9.16% Top Producing States: New Mexico and Wyoming accounted for approximately 73% of total production volume Highest YoY Growth: New Mexico at 8.02% Lowest Average WTI Price: Idaho at $63.60 Highest Average WTI Price: Louisiana at $81.37, driven by production concentration during peak pricing years (2022–2024) Lowest Price Year: 2020 with an average WTI price of $49.18.
